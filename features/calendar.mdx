The Calendar module is where you schedule and track everything happening across your church — Sunday services, cell group meetings, leadership retreats, baptism services, outreach events, and more. Events can be scoped to a specific congregation or group, assigned to a responsible member, and populated with participants drawn from your member list. The result is a church-wide activity record that keeps your entire leadership team aligned.

## Creating an event

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Calendar module">
    Click **Calendário** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new event">
    Click **Novo evento** in the top bar, or click directly on any empty day in the monthly calendar view to pre-fill the date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the core details">
    Enter the event **title** (required) and select the **type** from the dropdown. Set the **date** and, if applicable, the **time**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scope the event (optional)">
    Link the event to a **congregation** and/or a **group** to indicate where and for whom it is happening. Leave these blank for church-wide events.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign a responsible member (optional)">
    Select the member who is organizing or leading this event. Their name will appear on the event detail.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure recurrence (optional)">
    If this is a recurring activity — a weekly service, monthly prayer meeting, or annual retreat — toggle on the **Recorrente** option and set the recurrence pattern (weekly, monthly, etc.).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Salvar**. The event appears on the calendar immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Recurring events

Recurring events let you create a whole series of activities from a single form — useful for regular services, weekly cell meetings, or monthly leadership gatherings.

<Warning>
  When you **edit a recurring event**, the change may apply to the **entire series** — all past and future instances — depending on the edit option you choose. Review the confirmation dialog carefully before saving. If you only want to change one occurrence, select the "only this event" option when prompted.
</Warning>

Similarly, deleting a recurring event will prompt you to choose whether to remove only the selected occurrence or the entire series.

## Participants

You can attach a participant list to any event, drawn directly from your member records:

- **Add individually** — open the event, navigate to the Participants section, and search for a member by name.
- **Bulk add** — use the bulk participant feature to add multiple members at once, filtering by congregation or group to quickly select a relevant subset of your members.
- **Remove a participant** — click the remove icon next to any participant's name in the event detail view.

Participant lists give you a record of who attended or was expected at each event, which is useful for tracking engagement and follow-up.

## Calendar views

The Calendar module offers two complementary views:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Monthly calendar" icon="calendar" href="#">
    A traditional month grid with events shown on their dates. Click any event to open its details. Navigate between months using the arrow buttons. Best for visualizing your schedule at a glance.
  </Card>
  <Card title="List / agenda view" icon="list" href="#">
    A chronological list of all events within a selected period. Best for reviewing a long list of upcoming or past activities, exporting, or scanning events by type.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Switch between views using the toggle at the top of the Calendar module.

## Birthday counter

At the top of the calendar, Flock displays a **birthday counter** showing how many members have a birthday in the current month. Click the counter to open the full birthday list with each person's name and contact details, so you can reach out to celebrate with them.

<Note>
  Birthday data comes from the date of birth entered on each member's profile. Make sure dates of birth are filled in during member registration to keep this list accurate.
</Note>

## Filtering events

Use the filter bar to focus on specific events:

- **Congregation** — show only events for a specific location
- **Group** — show only events associated with a particular ministry, cell, or department
- **Date range** — define a custom from/to window
- **Event type** — filter by category

All filters can be combined, and active filters are shown as chips you can remove individually.

## PDF export

Export your calendar as a PDF by clicking **Exportar PDF** in the calendar header. The export respects your currently active filters — if you've filtered to a specific congregation and month range, only those events will appear in the document. This makes it easy to print a schedule for a specific ministry or prepare a handout for a leadership meeting.
