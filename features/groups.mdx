Groups let you organize your members beyond the congregation level. Where a congregation represents a physical location, a group represents a community of purpose — a worship ministry, a youth cell group, a finance department, a children's choir, a prayer team. Any way your church structures its internal life can be reflected in Flock's Groups module. Members can belong to multiple groups simultaneously, and groups feed directly into calendar events and analytics.

<Tip>
  You can scope a group to a specific congregation, or leave it church-wide. A worship ministry that spans all your sites would be church-wide; a cell group that meets at a specific branch would be tied to that congregation.
</Tip>

## Group types

When creating a group, you choose a **type** that categorizes it:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Ministério" icon="hands-praying" href="#">
    A ministry team — worship, prayer, evangelism, media, children's ministry, etc.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Célula" icon="people-group" href="#">
    A small group or cell group that meets regularly for discipleship or fellowship.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Departamento" icon="building" href="#">
    An administrative or operational department — finance, communication, facilities, etc.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Outro" icon="layer-group" href="#">
    Any other organizational unit that doesn't fit the above categories — choirs, sports teams, special committees, etc.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

The type appears as a label on each group card and is available as a filter, making it easy to view just your cells or just your departments.

## Creating a group

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Groups module">
    Click **Grupos** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a new group">
    Click **Adicionar grupo** (or the **+** icon). The creation form opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the name and type">
    Give the group a descriptive name and select its type from the dropdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign a congregation (optional)">
    Link the group to a specific congregation if it belongs to one location. Leave blank to make it church-wide.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign a responsible member (optional)">
    Select the group leader or responsible person from your existing members. This field accepts any active member.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Salvar**. The group is created and opens so you can immediately start adding members to its composition.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adding members to a group

Creating a group and populating it are two separate steps in Flock:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the group">
    From the Groups list, click on the group name to open its detail view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to the members section">
    The group detail view shows a **members** (composição) section listing everyone currently in the group.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add members">
    Click **Adicionar membro** and search for the person by name. Select them from the results and confirm. Repeat for each person you want to add.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove members">
    To remove someone, find them in the composition list and click the remove icon next to their name.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Only members already registered in the Members module can be added to a group. If someone is missing from the search results, make sure they have an active member record first.
</Note>

## Leader / responsible

The **responsible** member is the person accountable for the group — a ministry leader, cell facilitator, or department head. This person must already exist as a member in Flock. You can update the responsible at any time by editing the group.

The responsible's name appears on the group card in the list view and is available as a data point in group exports.

## Active and inactive groups

Groups can be marked as **active** or **inactive**:

- **Active groups** appear in all default list views, member assignment forms, calendar event forms, and analytics.
- **Inactive groups** are hidden from default filters but remain in the system so historical data is preserved — group membership records, past event associations, and analytics history are not lost.

Deactivate a group when it has concluded its season (for example, a short-term committee or a cell that dissolved) but you want to keep the record. You can reactivate a group at any time.

## Filtering groups

Use the filters at the top of the Groups list to narrow your view:

- **Name search** — type any part of a group's name
- **Congregation** — filter by the congregation the group is linked to
- **Type** — ministério, célula, departamento, or outro
- **Status** — active, inactive, or all

The summary bar at the top of the list shows the total count of groups broken down by type for a quick overview.

## Groups and the calendar

When creating a calendar event, you can associate it with a specific group. This means the event appears on that group's record, and group leaders can see their upcoming commitments in one place. See [Calendar](/features/calendar) for details.

## Groups and reports

Group membership data is included in Flock's analytics dashboard, letting you see how your members are distributed across ministries, cells, and departments. You can also export a group's member list as a PDF from the group detail view.
