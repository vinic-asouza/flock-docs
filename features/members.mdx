The Members module is the heart of Flock — it's where you store, search, and maintain records for every person in your church family. From basic contact details to baptism history, congregation assignment, and occupation, each member profile gives you a complete, living picture of your congregation. Everything you enter here feeds directly into reports, group rosters, calendar events, and the integration pipeline.

## Member records

Each member profile holds a rich set of information organized into logical sections:

  </Card>
  <Card title="Contact details" icon="address-book">

  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Search & filters

You can locate any member in seconds using text search and a layered filter system.

**Text search** matches across name, email, phone, WhatsApp, spouse name, and document number simultaneously — type any fragment and Flock narrows the list in real time.

**Basic filters** let you slice by:
- **Status** — active, inactive, or all
- **Congregation** — any congregation registered under your church
- **Role (cargo)** — filter by a specific ministry role

**Advanced filters** give you even finer control:

<Accordion title="All available advanced filters">

| Filter | Options |
|---|---|
| Gender | Masculino / Feminino |
| Marital status | Solteiro, Casado, Divorciado, Viúvo, Outro |
| Nationality | Any text value |
| State | Brazilian state (via IBGE) |
| City | City within selected state |
| Neighbourhood | Free text |
| Age range | Minimum and maximum age |
| Occupation | Free text |
| Birth date range | From / to |
| Baptism date range | From / to |
| Admission date range | From / to |

</Accordion>

Active filters appear as chips at the top of the list. Click the **×** on any chip to remove that filter without resetting the others.

You can also **sort** by any column — name, admission date, baptism date, and more — in ascending or descending order.

## List vs Grid view

The member list supports two display modes:

- **List view** — a compact table showing name, contact, congregation, and status in rows. Best for scanning large lists and comparing records side by side.
- **Grid view** — a card-based layout that shows each member as a visual card with their photo placeholder, name, and key details. Best for a more personal, at-a-glance overview.

Toggle between modes using the icons in the top-right of the member list. Flock remembers your preferred mode the next time you visit.

## Active and inactive members

Flock distinguishes between **active** and **inactive** members so your historical records are never lost:

- **Active members** appear in all default lists, reports, and analytics.
- **Inactive members** remain in the system with their full history intact, but they are hidden from default filters and do not count in standard demographic reports.

To change a member's status, open their profile and choose **Deactivar** or **Reativar**. Confirm the action in the dialog that appears. The status change takes effect immediately and is atomic — there is no risk of partial updates.

<Note>
  Deactivating a member does **not** delete their record. All their data — baptism dates, addresses, group memberships — is preserved and accessible if you filter by "Inactive" or "All."
</Note>

## Batch operations

If you need to register several new members at once — for example, after a membership class — use the **batch creation** feature. Click the batch icon next to the main "Add member" button, fill in each record in the multi-row form, and save all records in a single operation.

## CSV import

For larger migrations, Flock lets you import members from a CSV file:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare your CSV">
    Format your spreadsheet using the column order expected by Flock. Download the template from the import dialog if you need a starting point.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload and validate">
    Click the import icon (upload arrow) in the Members header. Select your `.csv` file (maximum 10 MB). Flock validates the file and shows a preview with any errors highlighted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fix errors">
    Correct any flagged rows in your spreadsheet, re-upload, and validate again until no errors remain.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the import">
    Click **Importar** to process the file. Flock shows a summary of how many records were created successfully.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The import respects your plan's member limit. If the file would push you over your limit, the import is blocked and you'll see a message telling you how many slots are available.
</Note>

## Birthday list

The Members module includes a dedicated birthday view so you never miss celebrating a church family member. Navigate to the **Aniversariantes** section to see all members whose birthday falls in the current month, along with their contact details so you can send a message or call directly.

<Tip>
  The Calendar module also displays a birthday counter at the top of the monthly view. Click it to open the same birthday list without leaving your calendar.
</Tip>

## PDF export

You can export member data as PDF in two ways:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Individual member profile" icon="file-pdf" href="#">
    Open a member's detail view and click **Exportar PDF** to download a formatted document with their full profile.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Filtered member list" icon="file-lines" href="#">
    Apply any combination of filters, then open the export menu and click **Exportar lista (PDF)**. Choose which fields to include before downloading.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Field selection lets you control exactly what appears in the exported list — you can include or exclude contact details, dates, congregation, role, and address fields to match your use case (sharing with elders, printing a directory, etc.).

A CSV export of the filtered list is also available from the same export menu.

## Member limit

<Note>
  Your subscription plan defines the maximum number of **active** members you can have at one time. The current count and your plan's limit are displayed in the app header so you always know where you stand. Inactive members do **not** count against your limit. Contact us to upgrade your plan if you need more capacity.
</Note>
