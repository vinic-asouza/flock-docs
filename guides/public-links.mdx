Flock supports two types of public forms that anyone can fill in without a Flock account. Each form is tied to a secure, token-based URL that you generate inside the app and then share with your community. Submissions flow directly into the relevant module — no manual data entry required on your end. This is one of the fastest ways to grow your member database and integration pipeline at the same time.

## Public Registration Link

The public registration link is designed for self-registration of new members. When someone fills out the form, Flock creates a full member record for that person, active and visible immediately in the Members module.

**How it works:**

- Your church has a unique registration URL containing a secure token (e.g., `.../public/registration/<token>`).
- You share this URL via WhatsApp, email, a QR code on a printed card, or anywhere else.
- The visitor opens the link in any browser — no app download or account needed.
- They fill in their details. Flock validates the data and, on submission, creates a new active member record directly.
- The submission respects your plan's member limit: if the limit is already reached, the form will be blocked and the visitor will be prompted to contact the church administration.

**Where to find it:**  
Go to **Configurações** (Settings) or look for the link management option in the Members section of your dashboard.

**Managing the link:**  
You can create multiple registration links, set a maximum number of uses (after which the link stops accepting submissions), set an expiry date, and deactivate a link at any time without deleting it.

## Public Integration Link

The public integration link works the same way as the registration link, but submissions go into the **Integration pipeline** instead of the Members module. This is ideal for visitor outreach — anyone who fills in the form becomes an integrante with status **Em andamento**, ready for your team to follow up.

**How it works:**

- Your church has a unique integration URL containing a secure token (e.g., `.../public/integration/<token>`).
- The visitor fills in their name, date of birth, and contact information. They can also select their expected congregation from a list of your active congregations.
- On submission, Flock creates a new integrante record. The **Mentor** and **Notes** fields are not part of the public form — your team assigns those after the record appears in the system.
- Because integrantes do not count against your member limit, this form is never blocked by plan limits.

**Where to find it:**  
Go to **Configurações** or look for the link management area inside the Integration module.

**Managing the link:**  
Just like the registration link, you can set maximum uses, an expiry date, and deactivate it at any time.

<Tip>
  Print the integration link as a **QR code** and display it on visitor cards, welcome signs, or at the church entrance. Visitors can scan it with their phone camera, fill in the form in under a minute, and appear in your integration list immediately — without any staff intervention.
</Tip>

## Token-based security

Every public link uses a **unique security token** embedded in its URL. Only people who have the link can access and submit the form. There are no publicly listed or discoverable form endpoints — possession of the specific URL is the only requirement.

If a link is ever shared in an unintended channel or you suspect misuse, **deactivate it** in Settings. The token becomes invalid immediately, and new submissions are blocked. You can then generate a fresh link to replace it.

---

## Rate limiting

To prevent spam and abuse, Flock applies **rate limits** to all public form submissions. This is enforced server-side and limits the number of submissions that can be accepted from the same IP address in a short time window. Legitimate visitors filling in the form normally will never hit these limits.

---

## Viewing submissions

Public form submissions are visible **immediately** in the respective module as soon as they are accepted:

- **Registration submissions** → appear in **Membros** as active members
- **Integration submissions** → appear in **Integração** as integrantes with status **Em andamento**

<Note>
  There is no moderation queue for public form submissions — records are created directly. If you prefer to review submissions before they are fully active, the integration pipeline is the better route, since integrantes require manual conversion to become members.
</Note>
